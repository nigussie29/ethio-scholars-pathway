## Why This Program Exists

Many Ethiopian students are academically strong but lack access to clear information, test preparation, application support, mentorship, and financial resources needed to compete for international scholarships. Ethio Scholars Pathway helps close that gap.

## What We Provide

- U.S. college application guidance
- SAT, ACT, TOEFL, or Duolingo English Test preparation support
- Essay and personal statement coaching
- Academic English support
- College research support
- Mentorship from educators and professionals
- Scholarship-readiness training
- Small financial support when funds are available

## Who Can Apply

Students may apply if they are:

- Ethiopian high school students in Grade 10, 11, or 12
- Academically strong and motivated
- Interested in studying in the United States
- Able to attend online classes consistently
- Supported by a parent or guardian
- Able to show financial need

## How Selection Works

Students are selected based on academic performance, entrance assessment, writing, motivation, financial need, leadership, and teacher recommendation.

## Program Structure

The first pilot program will serve 20 students for six months through weekly online classes, advising sessions, mentorship, writing practice, and college preparation activities.

## Donor Message

Your support can help a talented student pay for internet access, test preparation, application costs, document preparation, or emergency educational support.

## Closing Statement

Talent exists everywhere. Opportunity does not. Ethio Scholars Pathway exists to connect Ethiopian talent with global opportunity.

# About Us Page Draft

Ethio Scholars Pathway was created to support talented Ethiopian high school students who dream of studying in the United States but need guidance, preparation, and financial support.

The founder understands both Ethiopian education and the U.S. education system. With a background in mathematics, computer science, data analysis, and teaching, the program is designed to give students practical preparation, honest guidance, and a structured pathway toward scholarship opportunities.

The program begins with a small pilot group of committed students and grows through partnerships with Ethiopian schools, mentors, donors, educators, and college-advising organizations.
